## Goal
The Occupational Employment and Wage Statistics (OEWS) program, published by the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS), aims to provide comprehensive data on occupational employment and wage rates for wage and salary workers across nonfarm industries in the United States. The May 2024 OEWS estimates offer detailed insights into employment levels, wage distributions, and occupational trends by industry and geographic area. This data supports workforce planning, policy development, economic research, and career guidance.

## Gap Addressed
Prior to the release of the May 2024 OEWS estimates, there was a need for up-to-date, reliable occupational employment and wage data reflecting labor market conditions across U.S. regions and sectors. Existing datasets may lack granularity or timeliness, particularly for emerging occupations or rapidly changing industries. The OEWS fills this gap by delivering semiannual, nationally consistent estimates with detailed occupational classifications and geographic breakdowns.

## Method
The OEWS survey is a semiannual federal-state cooperative program that collects employment and wage data from a sample of nonfarm establishments across all 50 states and the District of Columbia. Data are collected through employer surveys and combined with administrative records, then weighted and benchmarked to produce estimates for over 800 occupations. The estimates are categorized by employment levels, hourly and annual wages (including percentiles), and are available by industry, occupation, and geographic area.

## Datasets and Metrics
**Datasets:** May 2024 OEWS estimates

**Metrics:** Employment level, hourly mean wage, annual mean wage, wage percentiles (10th, 25th, 50th, 75th, 90th), employment per 1,000 jobs, location quotient

## Results
The May 2024 OEWS data were released on April 2, 2025, and are accessible via the BLS website through interactive tools, tables, and charts. The dataset provides detailed occupational employment and wage estimates for over 800 occupations across all U.S. states, metropolitan areas, and industry sectors. Users can analyze wage distributions, compare regional employment concentrations, and track changes over time. The data are used by policymakers, researchers, educators, and job seekers to understand labor market dynamics. Technical notes confirm the methodological rigor and statistical reliability of the estimates. Updates to related publications, such as the Employment Projections program, incorporate the 2024 OEWS wage data for improved accuracy.

## Limitations
The OEWS survey does not cover self-employed workers, agricultural workers, or private household employees, limiting its scope. Estimates are based on a sample and may have sampling errors, particularly for small occupations or regions. Data are published semiannually, so they may not reflect real-time labor market changes.
